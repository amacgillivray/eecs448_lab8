24 Heures
du Mans

 

The Stuff of Legend

The 24 Hours of Le Mans is the premiere racing event of the FIA World Endurance Championship. Held in Le Mans, France, the race sends high-powered vehicles hurtling down 6 kilometers of French country roads to the town of Mulsanne, where they enter a heavy breaking zone before turning to right (west) and breaching the outskirts of Arnage. The famous Arnage corner points the cars back in a northernly direction, ultimately placing them back on a private racing track at the Porsche Curves. After just a few more corners, the cars complete the first 13.6 KM lap - about 8.5 miles - of the 24 hour race.

Tragedy for the Toyota Gazoo Racing team in 2016, as their car - set to win the race after just one more lap - dies outside of the pit lane.

A Cathedral
of Speed

In normal years, the race attracts fans of horsepower and gasoline from all over the world. With over 250,000 attendees in 2018, it is one of the largest sporting events both in France and the world.
